== Key takeaways

. *Native governance*: Every capability shown ships with OpenShift. No additional operators, no add-on licensing.

. *Namespace isolation*: Hard boundaries between tenants with no implicit inheritance. Silence is denial.

. *Group-based access*: Bind roles to Groups, not individual users. Offboarding is one command per group.

. *Defined blast radius*: ServiceAccount permissions are scoped to the minimum needed. Define the blast radius before a compromise, not after.

. *Separation of duties*: Auditor roles exclude Secrets by design. RBAC and quotas are independent governance gates.

. *Compliance trail*: Every API call is logged with user, verb, resource, and response code. Denied attempts are recorded.

. *GitOps-ready*: All role and binding definitions are declarative YAML. Stored in Git. Reviewed in pull requests. Applied by Kustomize or ArgoCD.

== Handling final questions

*"This seems more complex than VMware."*

"It is more _visible_, not more complex. Every permission you had in VMware existed -- they just were not easily inspectable. One command gives you every access grant in the cluster in a format you can version-control."

*"How do we migrate existing vCenter groups?"*

"OpenShift LDAP group sync mirrors Active Directory groups into OpenShift Groups. You configure a sync schedule, run the initial sync, and from that point your AD groups are available to bind in RBAC policies. The binding model is identical to what you saw today."

*"What about multi-cluster?"*

"Red Hat Advanced Cluster Management (RHACM) applies RBAC policies across a fleet of clusters from a single control plane. Everything in this demo scales to multi-cluster with RHACM's Policy engine."

== Next steps

. *Deploy it yourself*: The demo environment is ready to use.
+
[source,bash]
----
git clone https://github.com/jlmayorga/openshift-demos
cd openshift-demos/rbac-demo
./scripts/deploy.sh
----

. *Explore the YAML*: Every role, binding, and quota in this demo is in `install/`. Read the files alongside the scenarios to understand the mapping from concept to manifest.

. *Run the validation*: `./scripts/validate.sh` checks every RBAC grant demonstrated in this guide.

. *Try it on your own cluster*: The demo requires only an OpenShift 4.16+ cluster with cluster-admin access. No operators needed.
